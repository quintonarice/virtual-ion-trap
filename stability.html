Mathieu Stability Diagram

Mathieu Parameters

\[ a = \frac{4 Q U}{m r_0^2 \Omega^2} \] \[ q = \frac{2 Q V_{\mathrm{RF}}}{m r_0^2 \Omega^2} \]

Here \(U\) is the DC voltage, \(V_{\mathrm{RF}}\) is the RF amplitude, \(m\) is the ion mass, \(r_0\) is the trap radius, and \(\Omega\) is the RF angular frequency.

Sample Learning Activity

Exploring Stability in a Linear Paul Trap

Use the sliders to explore how the Mathieu parameters a and q depend on experimental control parameters.

  • Increase the RF voltage while keeping the RF frequency fixed. How does the operating point move in the stability diagram?
  • Increase the RF frequency while holding all voltages constant. Does the trap become more or less stable?
  • Change the ion mass while keeping the operating point inside the stability region. Which ions are easier to trap stably?
  • Find one combination of parameters that produces unstable motion. Which parameter change caused the instability?

Reflection: Using the equations above, explain why increasing the RF frequency can stabilize the trap even at higher voltages.
